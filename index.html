About

After graduating highschool in Colorado Springs, I atteneded the University of Colorado, Colorado Springs for two years and got my Private Pilot License, before moving to Fort Colling to complete my undergrad degree in Computer Science. I had heard great things about the program at CSU, which turned out to be accurate. I worked my way through college, ending up as a lead line cook of a fine dining restaurant at the Fort Collins Country Club, all while learning new programming languages, design concepts, and soft skills such as the scrum process. Looking for a change of pace, I moved to New Jersey to start working for Lockheed Martin as a test/systems engineer after graduation.

Moving forward I hope to keep developing and learning as an engineer. I am very interested about the machine learning field, and data science field. It seems that data is the modern day gold, but there are too many developing fields in tech to limit myself to one field just yet.

Projects

Trip Planning Application

I lead a team of five as the scrum master in the development of a mobile first, test driven application. In this application, users were able to access a database of locations, and create and modify a trip, anywhere in the world.

The code is not public, but some screenshots are available here: GitHub.

Twitter Clone App

In a series of android application projects, I created an app from scratch, that read from, and wrote to the twitter database using an API.

The code, and a video walkthrough can be found here: GitHub.

Recent Movies App

One of the other apps that I created was a movie viewer, taken from 'The Movie Database API', and displayed to an app.

The code, and a video walkthrough can be found here: GitHub.

TCP SYN Flood Attack

One of the most interesting projects I have completed was simulating a TCP SYN flood attack on a set of virtual machines. Cookies were then used to detect, and prevent the attack from initiating a DDoS attack. The results were then graphed and analyzed.

The code, and graphs can be found here: GitHub.

Apache Spark Map Reduce

One capstone project that I completed at my time at CSU was running a map reduce cluster on 6GB of data. My team chose to analyze where to move based on housing data and job posting data.

The code, and graphs can be found here: GitHub.
